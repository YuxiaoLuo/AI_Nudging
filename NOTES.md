# nudging - NOTES

## Working title
**Talking Shoppers Into Buying? Experimental Evidence on LLM-Driven AI Nudges**

## Core idea from Rain
The rapid integration of LLMs into online shopping platforms has transformed how consumers purchase products. Unlike traditional digital nudges, LLM-based conversational assistants introduce a new form of interactive AI nudging that fundamentally reshapes consumer experiences. The study aims to unveil the impact of LLM-based AI nudges on purchase decision-making.

## Initial material extracted from `AI nudges.pptx`

### Candidate positioning
The deck frames this project as one of the first empirical comparisons between:
1. **LLM-based conversational nudging**
2. **Traditional UI-based nudging** such as badges, rankings, or static recommendation cues
3. **Control condition** with no nudge or baseline interface

### Core theoretical intuition
LLM-based conversational nudges may differ from traditional nudges because they are:
- interactive rather than static
- adaptive rather than fixed
- personalized through dialogue rather than generic UI placement
- opaque in how recommendations are generated
- conversationally persuasive rather than visually suggestive

### Candidate mechanisms appearing in the slides
- perceived transparency
- perceived personalization
- trust in the recommendation mechanism
- cognitive load
- choice confidence
- decision quality
- purchase likelihood
- satisfaction

### Likely theoretical tension
The project seems to rest on a tradeoff:
- LLM nudges may increase persuasion, personalization, and ease of decision making
- but they may also reduce transparency and make influence harder for users to detect or evaluate

That tradeoff is promising. It can support a richer contribution than simply asking whether chatbot recommendations increase purchase.

## Preliminary research question space
- How do LLM-based conversational nudges influence purchase decisions compared with traditional UI nudges?
- Through what mechanisms do conversational AI nudges affect choice confidence, cognitive load, trust, and decision quality?
- Does reduced transparency attenuate or qualify the positive effects of conversational AI nudges?

## Recommended framing direction
A strong framing is not just “LLMs are new nudges.” A stronger framing is:
**LLM conversational agents constitute a qualitatively different form of choice architecture because they blend recommendation, interaction, and persuasion into a dynamic and opaque conversational process.**

This lets the paper speak to:
- AI nudging
- digital choice architecture
- conversational commerce
- human-AI decision support
- responsible platform design

## Recommended next artifact
