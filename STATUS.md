# nudging - STATUS

## Overview
This project develops a study on how LLM-based conversational AI nudges shape consumer purchase decision-making in online shopping environments. The working title is: **Talking Shoppers Into Buying? Experimental Evidence on LLM-Driven AI Nudges**.

## Current Focus
Turn the existing concept into a more execution-ready paper design by locking the main outcomes, simplifying the mechanism model, and aligning the manuscript blueprint with a clean first-paper strategy.

## Recent Progress
- Core project idea identified: compare LLM-driven conversational nudges with traditional UI-based nudges and a control condition.
- Working title anchored as `Talking Shoppers Into Buying? Experimental Evidence on LLM-Driven AI Nudges`.
- Initial framing extracted from `AI nudges.pptx`.
- The project appears to center on how conversational AI changes purchase likelihood, perceived personalization, cognitive load, confidence, trust, transparency, and decision quality.
- A conceptual distinction has now been documented between LLM conversational nudges and algorithm-generated recommendation badges in e-commerce choice architecture.
- An initial literature review plan, review memo, reading matrix, and synthesis memo have been created with emphasis on UTD24 and ABDC A* business-journal anchors.
- A running `questions_log.md` has now been created to capture open questions, uncertainties, and resolved decisions during autonomous iteration.
- A misaligned paper with a misleading filename was verified and removed from the usable literature set after metadata inspection.
- The current synthesis suggests the strongest positioning is a tradeoff model: conversational AI may improve personalization, reduce cognitive burden, and increase confidence, while also reducing transparency.
- The manuscript blueprint has now been materially sharpened: it specifies a cleaner tradeoff model, narrows the primary outcomes to purchase likelihood and perceived decision quality, demotes trust and satisfaction to secondary outcomes, and adds a compact first-pass hypothesis structure (H1-H8).
- `ai nudging on digital platform.docx` appears empty on disk right now, so it could not yet be used as a source for development.
